Work In Progress: Lab Break

More Images

Currently working on a mobile game where the character is trying to escape from the research lab it was created in. The aim is to make a combination of a platform and puzzle game.

The user controls are very simple, the character is constantly running, reversing direction whenever it hits an obstacle with the player only controlling when to make the character jump by tapping.

The character will also be able to drink potions along the way which will change its colour. It will need to do this in the correct order and with the correct timing to pass through doors that will only permit characters of a certain colour.

The game is a combination of platformer and puzzle game as the character's progress will depend on the accuracy and timing of jumping but also on collecting the objects at the right time and in the correct order.

VR: Ski VR

Play Store | More Images

Ski VR is a short VR demo built for Google Cardboard but also playable without a headset.

The player slides gracefully down a low-poly mountain between trees and alpine chalets steering by turning their head in the direction they want to go.

Ski VR was built in Unity with the Google Cardboard SDK and is available for Android on the Google Play store.

Puzzle: Color Hex

Play Store | iTunes | More Images

Color Hex is a mobile puzzle game for Android and iOS built and published using the Unity platform.

The player must try and match patterns by mixing colours together on a hexagonal grid. As the patterns get more complicated the puzzle becomes more difficult.

There are 2 play modes with a combined total of 222 levels.

Color Hex was built in Unity which supports exporting to both iOS and Android platforms.

Game: Super Dice

Play Store | More Images

My first attempt at using the Unity platform to create a game was a mobile version of the dice game Farkle.

Players take it in turn to roll 6 dice hoping to get high scoring combinations. As they build up a score they must choose whether to continue and risk everything or play it safe and bank the points they already have.

The app keeps track of all the points calculating the sometimes complicated combinations in which different dice rolls can score.

App also has a simple "just dice" mode where it can be used as a replacement for physical die.
